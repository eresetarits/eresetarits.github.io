About

I'm a postdoctoral scholar at the Center for the Ecology of Infectious Disease at the Odum School of Ecology at UGA. I focus on putting parasites into a community and ecosystem context. I'm interested in how parasite communities are structures across scales, respond to competition for hosts, and influence nutrient cycling. My work is primarily experimental, testing theory in the field.

Research Questions

  • How do parasites influence thermal limits of their hosts?
  • Do parasites significantly contribute to nutrient cycling?
  • Are there parasite-ecosystem feedbacks?
  • How is trematode colony composition influenced by nutrients?
  • What variables predict parasite communities across spatial scales?
  • Do keystone communities exist?
  • Do trematode colonies invest in more soldiers under higher invasion threat?
